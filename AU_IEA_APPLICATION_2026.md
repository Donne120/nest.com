## BRIEF STATEMENT OF THE PROBLEM

Africa faces a structural education crisis with roots deeper than infrastructure. **60% of 15–17 year-olds remain excluded from formal education.** Among those who do reach school, teacher shortages affect every country on the continent — and the education that exists produces graduates disconnected from labour markets.

Where digital education platforms exist, they fail Africa in predictable ways: they require credit cards, expensive per-seat licenses, always-on broadband, or English-only interfaces. Mobile Money — the financial infrastructure already serving over 400 million Africans — is completely ignored by global EdTech companies.

The deeper problem is not access alone. Every existing platform treats education as content consumption — a learner watches a video and the experience ends. Knowledge generated in classrooms never accumulates. The same questions are asked over and over by every new cohort. When a great teacher leaves a school, their knowledge leaves with them.

**Nest was built to solve all of this at once:** to give any African educator — with a smartphone and a $9 monthly mobile money payment — the infrastructure to reach thousands of learners, generate AI answers grounded in what was actually taught, and build a permanent, growing knowledge base that gets smarter with every cohort that passes through it.

## LONG-FORM 1 (~500 words)
### How the Innovation Contributes to Digital Transformation, AI Integration (Including Ethics), STI Infrastructure, and Skills Alignment with Industry

**Nest is the digital transformation layer that Africa's education system is missing.**

Every day, millions of African learners sit in classrooms where the quality of their education depends entirely on one variable: whether a qualified teacher is in the room. When the teacher is absent, learning stops. When the teacher leaves the school, their knowledge leaves with them. When a student asks a question at 10pm, there is no one to answer. Nest dissolves all three constraints simultaneously.

**AI Integration with Built-In Ethics**

Nest's AI tutoring system is architecturally designed to prevent the central ethical risk of AI in education: misinformation. When a learner asks a question, the platform does not consult a general-purpose language model drawing on unconstrained internet knowledge. It first extracts the exact transcript window from the video — the three minutes of content that was literally being taught at that moment — and constrains the AI to answer from that evidence alone. The AI tells learners what their teacher said, referenced to the second. If no transcript exists, the AI explicitly acknowledges this limitation rather than fabricating an answer.

This is not a feature — it is a structural ethical commitment. AI answers in Nest are always traceable to a human educator's original content. Educators review AI responses before they become official answers, ensuring human oversight is never removed from the learning relationship. This is a working model of responsible AI deployment in African education — not a policy document, but a running system.

**STI Infrastructure at Continental Scale**

The multi-tenant architecture means Nest does not need to be deployed institution by institution. A single deployment serves every school, TVET, and NGO on the continent simultaneously — each with its own isolated workspace, custom branding, and content library. What typically requires an IT department, server infrastructure, and months of procurement is replaced by a URL and a monthly mobile money payment. This is the difference between a point solution and continental infrastructure.

The platform automatically transcribes every uploaded video using Groq Whisper — a state-of-the-art multilingual speech model supporting over 100 languages. This makes African language content viable at scale for the first time, without manual labour.

**Skills Alignment with Industry**

Nest includes a built-in Applicant Tracking System (ATS) that integrates with Greenhouse, Lever, and Workable — the dominant hiring platforms used by employers across Africa and globally. This creates a direct pipeline: a learner completes a vocational course, earns a verifiable digital certificate with a permanent public link, and their profile moves directly into an employer's talent pipeline. The analytics system identifies which video moments generate the most learner confusion — providing TVET instructors with evidence-based data on precisely where curriculum alignment with industry needs to improve.

**Green and Accessible by Design**

Nest eliminates physical materials — no printed textbooks, no travel to training centres, no paper certificates. The MoMo payment integration means financial inclusion is structural, not optional. A learner in a rural area with a feature phone can access world-class education with the same transaction they use to buy airtime. Nest does not require Africa to wait for infrastructure that was promised and never arrived. It works with what Africa already has.

## LONG-FORM 3 (~500 words)
### Description of the Innovation: How It Functions, Implementation Approach, Revenue Model, and Alignment with CESA, CTVET-34, STISA-2034, and the AU Continental AI Strategy

**How Nest Functions**

Nest is a multi-tenant Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) platform. An educational institution — a TVET school, university department, NGO, or independent educator — registers an organisation in under five minutes. They receive a custom workspace with their branding, upload video-based course content, invite learners, and begin teaching immediately.

The learner experience is built around five integrated systems that together create something no single existing platform provides:

**(1) Video Learning with Timestamped Intelligence.** Learners watch videos through a custom player that shows — as visual markers on the timeline — exactly where questions have been asked across all previous cohorts. Pausing at any moment and submitting a question generates a query stamped to that precise second in the lesson.

**(2) AI Tutoring Grounded in Lesson Content.** Every uploaded video is automatically transcribed with timestamped segments via Groq Whisper. When a question is submitted, the AI reads the actual transcript window around that timestamp and generates a response traceable to what the educator taught. Educators review and approve responses — human oversight is embedded by architecture, not added as a policy afterthought.

**(3) Collaborative Assignments with Automated Workflow.** Educators create individual or group assignments within courses. The platform automatically forms groups, assigns portions to each member, auto-schedules two structured meetings (a kickoff session and a pre-submission review), and merges completed work into one document when all members submit. This replicates a supervised, project-based learning environment at zero marginal cost per group.

**(4) Assessment and Portable Certification.** Quizzes with multiple choice, true/false, and short-answer questions are attached to videos. Learners who complete all content receive a digitally verifiable certificate with a permanent, shareable public link — usable immediately on CVs and professional profiles.

**(5) Analytics and Dropout Prevention.** Per-learner progress tracking, at-risk flags for inactive learners, top confusion timestamps per video, and a benchmarking system comparing institution performance against the platform average.

**Implementation Approach**

Nest is infrastructure, not a project. Implementation for any institution requires no IT staff, no installation, and no procurement cycle. An educator registers, brands their workspace, and uploads their first video in one session. The grant funds will be used to: (1) expand the platform's capacity and reliability for 10,000+ concurrent learners; (2) build partnerships with TVET institutions and ministries of education in target AU member states; (3) develop localised content templates aligned with national curricula; and (4) provide onboarding support for the first 100 institutions in the grant cohort.

**Revenue Model**

Nest charges institutions, not learners: $9/month (Starter), $29/month (Professional), $79/month (School/Institution). All payments are via MTN Mobile Money, Orange Money, or bank transfer — no credit card required. Nest retains 100% of subscription revenue; learners access their institution's content at no additional cost.

**Alignment with Continental Frameworks**

*CESA:* Nest addresses all three CESA pillars — access (MoMo-native pricing reaches the financially excluded), quality (AI constrained to educator content maintains pedagogical integrity), and relevance (ATS integration connects education directly to employment outcomes).

*CTVET-34:* The assignment engine and quiz system are designed for practical skills validation — the pedagogical core of TVET. Educators attach assignments to specific competencies and track mastery across cohorts, generating verifiable evidence of skills development at scale.

*STISA-2034:* Nest builds African STI capacity by enabling educators to create and distribute locally relevant digital learning content — amplifying African teachers' own knowledge at continental scale rather than importing foreign curricula.

*AU Continental AI Strategy:* Nest is a working implementation of responsible AI in African education. The AI operates within educator-defined content boundaries, requires human review before answers are published, and generates no output untraceable to verified source material. It is a replicable ethical AI model that can inform national education policy across AU member states.
